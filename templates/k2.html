üìñ About the Mission

After a mechanical failure in 2013, the Kepler mission was reinvented as the K2 mission. Rather than observing a single region of the sky, K2 used solar radiation pressure to stabilize the telescope, allowing observations of different sky regions during 80-day campaigns.

This innovative approach allowed observation of a greater variety of stars and planetary systems, while continuing to use the photometric transit method that made Kepler successful.
