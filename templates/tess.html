üìñ About the Mission

The Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS), launched in April 2018, represents the new generation of exoplanet hunters. Unlike Kepler which observed a fixed region, TESS scans almost the entire sky, monitoring the brightest and closest stars to Earth.

With its four wide-field cameras, TESS observes sky sections for 27 days, allowing detection of planet transits around stars we can study in detail with ground-based and space telescopes. The mission continues to be active and makes new discoveries daily.

üìä Data Format

To use our model with TESS data, your CSV file must contain:

  • Light flux columns (PDCSAP or SAP flux)
  • Time series data with 2-minute or 20-second cadence
  • Minimum 200 data points recommended
  • The model handles data gaps between sectors

CSV structure example:

FLUX_1,FLUX_2,FLUX_3,...,FLUX_N

üî¨ TESS Advantages

The TESS mission offers several unique advantages for exoplanet research:

  • Observation of brighter and closer stars
  • Coverage of almost the entire sky
  • Optimal targets for follow-up observations
  • Public data accessible in near real-time
  • Synergy with ground-based telescopes and JWST

üéØ The Future with TESS

TESS continues its extended mission, observing certain sky regions again to detect longer-period planets. TESS data also feeds preparation of observations with the James Webb Space Telescope and future extremely large ground-based telescopes.
