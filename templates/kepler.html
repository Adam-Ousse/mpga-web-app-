üìñ About the Mission

Launched in March 2009, the Kepler Space Telescope revolutionized our understanding of exoplanets. For nearly a decade, it observed a fixed region of the sky, continuously monitoring the brightness of over 150,000 stars.

The mission used the photometric transit method: when a planet passes in front of its star (as seen from Earth), it causes a slight decrease in the star's brightness. This is the signature that our AI model is trained to detect.

üìä Data Format

To use our model with Kepler data, your CSV file must contain:

  • Light flux columns (normalized flux)
  • Continuous time series data
  • Minimum 200 data points recommended
  • Numerical values (NaN accepted and will be cleaned automatically)

CSV structure example:

FLUX_1,FLUX_2,FLUX_3,...,FLUX_N
0.9998,1.0001,0.9997,...,1.0003
0.9995,1.0002,0.9999,...,1.0001
